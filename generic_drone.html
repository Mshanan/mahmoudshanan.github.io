Quanser Aero 2

The aim of this project was to design a cascaded PID controller to control the pitch and yaw of both configurations of the Quanser Aero 2 platform (1-DOF Bi-copter and 2-DOF Helicopter), and evaluate the feasability of developing a new 2-DOF quadcopter configuration. The performance of PID control in UAVs was evaluated through hardware-in-loop simulation, using MATLAB/Simulink and QUARC real-time control software.

The Quanser Aero 2 is a fixed platform used in research and development of control systems for UAVs. It consists of two brushless DC motors and rotors generating thrust allowing the system to pitch and yaw. It is also equipped with 4 encoders, a gyroscope, accelerometer, and magnetometer, measuring pitch and yaw angles, and rotor speed.

Key Findings

• It was not possible to control both pitch and yaw simultaneously in the bi-copter configuration, unlike the helicopter configuration.
• Pitch control in both configurations was almost identical, with fast time response, low steady-state error, and small oscillations.
• For bi-copter yaw control, time response was thirty times slower than the helicopter as both rotors were horizontal compared to the helicopter where one rotor was vertical.
• Mathematical and mechanical approaches were proposed to attempt controlling the system as a quadcopter, yet they were both unsuccessful. This implied that the Quanser Aero 2 could not be controlled as a quadcopter.
