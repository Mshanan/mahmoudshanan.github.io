Autonomous Buggy

The main aim of this project was to design an autonomous microcontroller-based buggy, which can autonomously follow a white line around a track using infrared sensors. It was part of my second year embedded systems group project during my bachelors.

• DC motors were first characterised, and a gearbox ratio was then chosen according to the maximum torque required to climb up slopes, while maintaining a relatively fast pace.
• Three different sensors were characterised and compared. The TCRT5000 (infrared sensor) was chosen due to its high dynamic range and low ambient current, meaning that the ambient light hardly affected the readings.
• The chassis of the buggy was designed on SolidWorks, and was lazer-cutted.
• The sensor's circuit was designed and simulated on Multisim, tested on a stripboard, then a PCB was designed on Altium, and printed.
• The control algorithm of the buggy was written in C++ (Mbed library), using object-oriented programming methods to read data from the infrared sensors, and send control signals (PWM) to the DC motors of the buggy, so forth performing 5 different manoeuvres: going forward, uphill, downhill, turning right and left, and stopping.
